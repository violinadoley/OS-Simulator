Producer Consumer Problem

The producer-consumer problem is a classic synchronization problem in computer science and operating systems. It involves coordinating the actions of two different types of processes or threads: producers and consumers. The goal is to ensure that producers can safely and efficiently produce items, and consumers can consume those items without conflicts or inconsistencies. In this problem, there are one or more producer processes/threads that generate data items, and one or more consumer processes/threads that consume those items. There is typically a shared buffer or queue that serves as the intermediate storage for the produced items until they are consumed.
